Campaign type: Recruiting firms

Write to the recruiter or staffing partner as a placement channel for their clients.

Rules:
- Write in first person as Manmit using "I".
- Do not pitch data engineering services to the recruiting firm's own internal team.
- Do not sound like a job application.
- Make the fit easy to scan: senior ETL / data engineering contractor, Informatica / IICS, SQL, Python, Airflow, dbt, Snowflake, migration, production support, enterprise data environments.
- Use one real company signal if available, but keep recruiter emails lighter on internal technical diagnosis than direct-buyer emails.
- Mention contract searches, placements, or the kinds of roles that are a fit when useful.
- End with a simple CTA asking whether a quick conversation makes sense or whether someone else on the team handles these searches.

Tone:
- Direct
- Senior
- Specific
- No hype
- No generic admiration

Avoid:
- "I was impressed by your company"
- "I typically work through recruiting partners"
- "This aligns well"
- Third-person references to Manmit
